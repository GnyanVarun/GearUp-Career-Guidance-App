Introduction

A Pediatrician, or Pediatric Physician, is responsible for performing routine health assessments of children to check for injuries, illnesses or disabilities. Their duties include communicating with patients and their parents to help them maintain healthy habits, administering vaccinations for common viruses or allergies and referring patients to specialists based on their needs.

Educational requirements

Education requirements for the career of a Pediatrician consist of an undergraduate degree followed by a four-year degree from an accredited medical school. A three-year residency program from an accredited pediatric program is also required. The candidate must complete and pass the national board exam in order to obtain a proper license to practice medicine on children and young adults. The candidate will also need to obtain recertification every seven years
