Introduction

Automotive engineers design the systems and mechanisms of cars. These professionals ensure that automotive designs and systems conform to budgeted quality and cost specifications. Similar to other types of mechanical engineers, automotive engineers analyze potential problems in design and work to solve those problems, design and retest prototypes of cars and car parts, and oversee the manufacturing of automotives. Computers are a big part of how automotive engineers do their jobs.

What do they do?

  • Preparing design specifications.
  • Researching, developing and producing new vehicles or vehicle subsystems.
  • Using computerized models to determine the behavior and efficiency of a vehicle.
  • Investigating instances of product failure.
  • Preparing cost estimates for current or new vehicles.
  • Assessing the safety and environmental components of an automotive project.
  • Creating plans and drawings for new vehicle products.

Key skills required

To be a successful automotive engineer, you will need a number of both hard and soft skills. These include:

  • Data analysis skills:Automotive engineers are responsible for performing and analyzing various automobile tests. These professionals must be able to efficiently and effectively read and interpret data to improve vehicle performance and production.
  • Problem-solving skills:Engineers in the automotive industry must be able to think creatively and originally to solve specific vehicle-related problems and create new solutions.
  • Technical skills:Automobile engineers must have a variety of technical skills related to this industry, including strong working knowledge of automobile maintenance and function.
  • Commercial Awareness:These professionals must stay abreast of industry trends as well as the latest in automobile technology and regulations.
  • Teamwork skills:Many automobile engineers work as a team to complete projects. This requires professionals to have strong teamwork skills and know how to effectively work with other individuals on a daily basis.

Educational requirements

An automotive engineer’s career training begins with a four-year bachelor’s degree from an accredited college or university. This is generally the basic requirement for entry-level jobs in the career. While pursing their bachelor’s degree, students take classes in a range of fields, including physics, mathematics, design and computer science. Certain programs also require core courses in the humanities, in areas such as English or philosophy. The first two years typically consist of foundational classes in math and science, while the last two or three years consist of more engineering-specific courses. Aspiring automotive engineers should pursue a degree in mechanical engineering or a related engineering field.
