Introduction

Chartered Accountants Provide Trustworthy Information About Financial Records. This Might Involve Financial Reporting, Taxation, Auditing, Forensic Accounting, Corporate Finance, Business Recovery And Insolvency, Or Accounting Systems And Processes.

What do they do?

  • Preparing financial management reports, including financial planning and forecasting.
  • Advising on tax and treasury issues.
  • Negotiating terms with suppliers.
  • Liaising with internal and external auditors and dealing with any financial irregularities as they arise.
  • Producing reports and recommendations following internal audits or public-sector audits.
  • Preparing financial statements, including monthly and annual accounts.

Key skills required

  • Organizing meticulously to keep financial documents in order.
  • Problem-solving to determine where errors might be occurring or to suggest ideas that would benefit the client, such as ways to limit tax liability.
  • Communicating well with others both orally and in writing so that people understand their options and your findings.
  • Committing to life-long learning to keep up with governmental and economic changes.
  • Being aware of financial situations and regulations in other countries since the global economy is increasingly becoming a concern to all businesses.
  • Employers of Chartered Accountants demand confidentiality and accuracy.

Educational requirements

ICAS is the governing entity that issues the chartered accountant designation and determines the requirements. There are multiple pathways to becoming a chartered accountant. Largely, the chartered accountant qualifications vary by country, professional experience and education level. The chartered accountant status is a bit more complex than obtaining certified public accountant status. There are four options for obtaining chartered accountant status: Five years of training from an approved provider directly upon leaving high school, Three years of training from an approved provider after graduating from college, Finance professionals with either a university degree or five years of professional experience, The Pathways route, which allows individuals with other accounting certifications to obtain chartered accountant status.
