Introduction

Computer and information research scientists design innovative uses for new and existing computing technology.

What do they do?

  • Oversees the computer development lifecycle.
  • Builds and optimizes computer software and hardware.
  • Conducts experiments and analyzes the results.
  • Develops machine learning and artificial intelligence solutions.
  • Creates predictive models and identifies business strategies.

Key skills required

  • Knows common programming languages and frameworks.
  • Excels at business intelligence and has strong operational skills.
  • Collaborates with colleagues and has strong interpersonal skills.
  • Explains technical information clearly and has strong presentation skills.
  • Communicates effectively and has strong verbal and written communication skills.
  • Works well independently and has strong time management and prioritization skills.
  • Enjoys taking on new projects and has strong problem-solving, critical thinking, and analytical skills.

Educational requirements

  • A bachelor’s degree in computer science or a related major is required.
  • A master’s degree or Ph.D. in computer science or a related major is strongly preferred.
  • At least [number] years of software engineering experience is required.
