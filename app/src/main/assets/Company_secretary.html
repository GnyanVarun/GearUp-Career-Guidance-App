Introduction

A company secretary's job is primarily on recording minutes during a board meeting. Company secretaries perform various administrative tasks in a company as they are tasked to keep records of the company's decisions and disseminate them to other board members. They are in charge of regulating the company in terms of its compliance with the regulatory and statutory requirements. If a company secretary finds an area of the company that needs improvement, it is also their job to advise the board of directors that will allow them to fulfill their goal as a company. They are a very important member of the company because they oversee the corporation's processes and decisions as well as counseling them for further development.

What do they do?

  • Filing annual returns at Companies House. Other documents which must be filed include the directors’ report and auditors’ report (unless the company is exempt), and financial statements, including details of the company’s assets and liabilities.
  • Arranging meetings of the directors and the shareholders. This responsibility will involve the issue of proper notices of meetings, preparation of agenda, circulation of relevant papers and taking and producing minutes to record the business transacted at the meetings and the decisions taken.
  • Informing Companies House of any significant changes in the company’s structure or management, for example the appointment or resignation of directors.
  • Establishing and maintaining the company’s registered office as the address for any formal communications. Ensuring that all the company’s business stationery carries its name, registered number, country of registration and registered address. These details must also appear on the company website, emails, order forms and invoices.
  • Ensuring the security of the company’s legal documents, including for example, the certificate of incorporation and memorandum and articles of association.

Key Skills required

  • Strong administrative skills and an aptitude for using IT software.
  • Commercial awareness.
  • Meticulous attention to detail.
  • Interpersonal skills.
  • Influencing skills.
  • Excellent organisation and time management.
  • The ability to take the initiative.
  • A flexible and practical approach to work.
  • Discretion and diplomacy.

Educational requirements

It is possible to become a company secretary with either school leaver or degree-level qualifications. Entry-level company secretary vacancies, sometimes known as assistant or trainee roles, often require an undergraduate degree, but a few are open to school leavers. Where a degree is requested, subjects related to law, finance and business are advantageous but not always essential.
