Introduction

Recruited by the Government of India, on the recommendation of Union Public Service Commission (UPSC), Indian Revenue Service Officer is a career diplomat serving in the formulation of domestic direct tax policy. In addition to this, they also work at the headquarters of the Ministry of Health Affairs, and Prime Minister's office. IRS Officers also lead the Regional Income Tax Offices throughout the country and hold positions in the President's Secretariat and several ministries on deputation.

What do they do?

Indian Revenue Service deals with the finance of the country, which includes foregin and domestic income tax legislation. The roles and responsibilities of an IRS Officer includes framing and implementation of policies which govern India’s domestic and foreign tax. The major work responsibilities of an IRS officer are representing India in its embassies, high commissions, consulates, and permanent missions to international organisations like the United Nations; protecting India’s national interests in the country one is posted, handling policy matters in respect of investigation of tax evasion, updating, resolving and maintaining the relevant laws (through the ITA Division), administering the direct tax policy (through its field offices across the country); conclude agreements on several issues with the authorities of the receiving state; and augmenting consular facilities to Revenues and Indians abroad.
