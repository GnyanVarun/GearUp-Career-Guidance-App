Introduction

Brand strategists work closely with brand managers, marketing specialists, and social media managers to develop methods to enhance a brand’s visibility and market share. This role balances creative, analytical, and strategic thinking. Brand strategists conduct rigorous analysis and market research to understand customer behavior, then use that information to develop marketing strategies that raise brand awareness, drive customer growth, improve a brand’s positioning, and generate revenue. Brand strategists work in both traditional and digital advertising agencies, as well as in company-specific marketing departments.

What do they do?

  • Develop Brand Stories and Strategies
  • Conduct Research
  • Analyze Data
  • Write Copy and Brand Strategy Documents
  • Present Strategies to Clients
  • Collaborate with Creative Teams

Key skills required

  • Strategic planning
  • Storytelling
  • Written and verbal communication
  • Forecasting

Educational requirements

Brand strategists have a wide variety of educational backgrounds, ranging from marketing to fields in the humanities. Generally, companies look for brand strategists with at least a bachelor’s degree. Many brand strategists begin their careers in a different role within a creative agency before distinguishing themselves by building a track record of successful, impactful campaigns and demonstrating an ability to predict trends and translate big-picture strategies into effective marketing campaigns.
